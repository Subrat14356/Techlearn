History of Computers

1. Abacus

The word "abacus" comes from the Greek word "abax" which means "border of cover" or "a table".

The abacus was one of the earliest calculating devices, made of a frame with rods and beads.

Calculations like addition and subtraction can be performed by moving beads along the rods.

Time period: Ancient times (origin around 2400 BCE)

2. John Napier's Bones

In the year 1617, John Napier, a Scottish mathematician, invented Napier's Bones.

It consisted of numbered rods (originally made of bone or ivory) marked with numbers that could be used for calculations.

This device made multiplication and division of large numbers much easier by using a system of lattice multiplication.

Year: 1617

3. Pascaline

In 1642, Blaise Pascal developed a mechanical calculator called the Pascaline.

It was one of the world's first adding machines, capable of performing additions and subtractions.

The device could handle numbers up to 999,999.999 through a series of interconnected wheels.

Year: 1642

4. Difference Engine

In the 1800s, Charles Babbage designed the Difference Engine.

This mechanical device was designed to perform mathematical calculations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Babbage later worked on the Analytical Engine in 1833, which had many features of modern computers.

Because of these groundbreaking designs, Charles Babbage is often called the Father of Computers.

Years: Difference Engine (1820s), Analytical Engine (1833)
